A Brief History of Bitcoin:


The intent of this dashboard is to provide a brief synopsis of some of the major Bitcoin historical events while simultaneously showing the price of Bitcoin at that period.

The first scene will contain the overall price graph alongside 11 annotations/steps. The order of the steps are from left to right in historical order. The user can click on any of them, but the intent is to begin with the first one and move down the order. As the user clicks on each step, the graph will update for some of the steps based on the relative time, and the appropriate annotation will be displayed in the updated chart.

The visualization uses a set range dates which filters the data and re-renders the graph when clicking on a step. I did this to attempt to look at the price of Bitcoin as some historical events occurred. At the end you can see the overall price graph and see if you can remember where those events happened.

The scenes will end 20 seconds after hitting the final step ETF. This will transition back to the original overall graph with no annotations.

Patent Application


August 15, 2008
Kin, Vladimir Oksman, and Charles Bry file an application for an encryption patent.

Many believe this to be linked to Bitcoin due the time coincide, but they deny any involvement with the project.

WebSite Registered

August 18, 2008
Bitcoin.org is registered at anonymousspeech.com by whom some believe to be Satoshi Nakamoto.

WhitePaper is Published

October 31, 2008
The official white paper for the design of Bitcoin was published and authored by Satoshi Nakamoto titled Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System.

The eight-page outline shows how the Byzantine Generals problem, previously unsolved for decades, and issues with double spending have been solved by this new Bitcoin invention.

Bitcoin's V0.1 Released

January 9, 2009
Version 0.1 of Bitcoin is released, and shortly after the first transaction is completed on January 12, 2009.

First Retail Transaction

May 22, 2010
The first retail purchase using bitcoin was completed. During this period Bitcoin was thought as a computer science experiment and only few enthusiast were involved with it. Needless to say that bitcoin had very little value, and to be accepted as a currency was a major feat.

10,000 bitcoins were used to purchase two pizzas in Jacksonville, Florida. This marks the start of bitcoin as a real currency.

BitPay Launch

May, 2011
BitPay launches as a merchant service provider that enables business to receive bitcoin as a form of payment. This strengthen the resolve and vision of Bitcoin becoming a currency.

BitPay has become the largest service provider in this category working with over 60,000 merchants.

Bitcoin Surge

November, 2013
Bitcoins sees a surge in price, going over $1,000 USD per bitcoin for the first time.

During this time Bitcoin saw an increase in media attention, and speculation as its price increased exponentially. This period also marks a dark shadow in Bitcoins history, as it become a top form of payment in the dark web specially used in the silk road.

Mt. Gox Hack

February , 2014
Mt.Gox, the largest CryptoCurrency exchange at the time announces that they have been hacked, and report the lose of over 850,000 bitcoins. This destroyed confidence leading to the price decline of Bitcoin for many months to follow.

Bitcoin entered the longest bear market it has experienced, loosing over 80% of value from its all time high in December 2014.

BlockChain Research

June 24, 2015
NASDAQ the world's second largest financial stock exchange begins trials on blockchain-backed transactions.

2015 marks the year where many institutions begin looking at the technology that runs Bitcoin (The BlockChain). MIT Media Lab launched the MIT Digital Currency Initiative to research future use cases of BlockChain technologies, and improve Bitcoin.

Bitcoin Futures

December 18, 2017
CME Group Inc. launches Bitcoin futures, soon followed by the CBOE Futures Exchange and Cantor Exchange.

The news of Bitcoin futures, and media attention bring a bullish market into the CryptoCurrency space which propels bitcoin to reach an all time high price of $19,783.06.

Bitcoin ETF

June 2, 2018
A bearish sentiment fell over the CryptoCurrency seeing a sharp decline in value over the months after the release of the Bitcoin futures.

The CBOE Global Markets filed a notice to the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) for Bitcoin Exchange Traded Fund(ETF) approval. The current market waits with optimism on the idea of a potential Bitcoin ETF.
